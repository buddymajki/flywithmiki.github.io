I am a visiting pilot. Can I legally fly in Switzerland?

Yes, if you fulfill the following criterias:

  • IPPI 4/5 license
  • Liability insurance (CHF 1M coverage minimum)
  • (recommended: accident insurance with coverage for paragliding)

Flying in the Alps could be demanding if you do not know the local sites, rules and weather phenomenas. I highly recommend you, to get in contact with local pilots / clubs / schools before your arrival, who could give you hints and tips. 

In case you do not fulfill the criterias above, or you would like to fly in a controlled environment independently from your skills, you could join me on an XC day, or schooling day. Contact Miki

For more information about the legal requirements, visit the website of the Swiss Association.

I moved/living in Switzerland and I have a foreign license (IPPI, APPI). May I fly?

If you are living in Switzerland, then you will need a Swiss paragliding license from the SHV (Swiss Paragliding Association), and a liability insurance for paragliding with at least CHF 1M coverage. Depending on your foreign license there are different scenarios. This is the way (official link):

  • IPPI5 license - simplified route: Registration with SHV, Theory Exam
  • IPPI4 license: Registration with SHV, Theory exam, Demonstrating your flying skills to a SHV certified Instructor, Do some high flights under the supervision of an instructor (if needed), Practical exam. 
  • IPPI 3/2/1: you have to do the complete training under the supervision of a Swiss instructor
  • APPI:  you have to do the complete training under the supervision of a Swiss instructor 

I can help you to get your Swiss License as soon as possible, independently from your level. Contact me to discuss the details
