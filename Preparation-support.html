Preparation support for your flying day

Plan your next day accordingly! Safety begins at home, and thorough preparation is key. Make informed decisions by checking the latest meteorological forecasts. Our preparation page supports you with essential information for both meteorological conditions and airspace considerations. You can find further links at the useful links section. It's crucial to stay ahead of the curve and prioritize safety before taking flight. Explore the resources available here to ensure a comprehensive pre-flight preparation. Make it a habit to check the Meteo and Airspace information every time before you embark on your journey into the skies. Your commitment to preparation is an integral part of a safe and enjoyable flying experience.

The "Meteo 5 point check" what we use for our daily preparation is based on the SHV's "Decision making strategy".

Do not forget: YOU are the pilot, YOU make your decisions! It is your way.

Front

Checkpoints Alarm signals
Textprognosis Front (Kaltfront, Okklusion, Störung, Kaltluft, Sturmtief, Westlage)
Precipitation forecast Coherent precipitation zone is approaching my region
Isobar maps Front is approaching, West wind situation
Meteo-maps Rain / storm symbols on the map
Temperature forecast Cold air moves towards me, T drop in the forecast
source: ​https://www.dwd.de/ source: https://meteo.ch

Föhn

Checkpoints Alarm signals
Textprognosis N-Alps: Föhn, Süd-föhn, Südwind, Föhntendenz   S-Alps: Nordföhn, Nordwind
Wind forecasts (2000m AMSL, 800m AGL)  The Föhn-channels are visible
Wind forecasts (3000m AMSL, 4000m  AMSL)  Wind in the Alpine arc meets the mountain range at a right angle. (Note: Does not apply to shallow foehn conditions!)
Pressure difference (N-S) Well pronounced overpressure or increasing pressure tendency on the LUV side of the Alps
Date Labilization due to warming during the day (especially spring & Summer)
Place The valley starts with a Pass in the main Alpine ridge (Föhntäler!)
Prévitemp (vertical T gradient forecast)  Air mass on the LUV side at the top and above the pass is cooler: from 1-2 °C. In the case of "shallow-föhn", this area above the pass can be small.
Temperature forecast (~2000m AMSL) Colder airmass on the LUV side of the alps
Föhn index from Meteo Stations Showing the curre​nt situations. 0: No Föhn, 1: Föhntendency, 2: Föhn

source: https://profiwetter.ch

source: https://geo.admin.ch, data: swisstopo, MeteoSwiss

Überregional wind (nation-wide)

Checkpoints Alarm signals
Textprognosis Mässiger Wind aus Westen, Nordwesten, Südwesten, Bise, Nordwind, Ostwind, böiger Wind
Wind prognosis at different altitudes Too strong winds
Thopography of the site Terrain, nation-wide wind canalizing potential
West-East pressure forecast >2 hPa pressure difference, increasing tendency

source: https://profiwetter.ch

source: https://xctherm.com/icon

Regional wind

Checkpoints Alarm signals
Text forecast Mässiger oder starker Talwind
Date April-October, afternoons
Place​ (alpine pump maps, turbulent zone maps) Alpine pump regions, Turbulent regions
Wind forecast (10, 800m AGL Strong valley winds in long-wide valleys, Nation-wide wind could increase the valley winds
Terrain Venturi effect

Storms and precipitation

Checkpoints Alarm signals
Text prognosis Keywords: (Wärme-/Hitze-)Gewitter, Tagesgangwetter, Schauer, Überentwicklungen, Labile Luft
Isobar map Shallow deep pressure situation
Precipitation forecast Patchy pattern with locally high rainfall intensity
Emagram Cloud development, Cb forming potential, CAPE (Convective Available Potential Energy)
